Mole Mash

1. Introduction

This tutorial will show you how to design a game where a mole moves once every half-second. If it is touched, the score increases by one, and the phone vibrates. Pressing restart resets the score to zero.

Connect using the companion app so you can test this on your mobile device. If you don't know how to connect, click here.

Click here for the original tutorial.

2. Add a Canvas

Click on "Drawing and Animation". Click and drag a Canvas to your Viewer. Go to Properties and set the height to 300px and the width to 300px.

3. Add a Sprite

Still in "Drawing and Animation", click and drag an ImageSprite to your Canvas. Go to Properties and download this image to your computer, then upload it to the Picture property.

4. Add a Label

Click on the User Interface drawer and drag a Label component to your Viewer. Go to Properties and change your Label's text to "Score: --"

5. Add a Button

Again in User Interface, click and drag a Button to your Viewer. Go to Properties and change your Button's text to Reset"

6.Add a Sound

Click Sensors and click and drag a Sound component to your viewer. It should appear under "Non-Visible Componenets".

6.Add a Clock

Click Media and click and drag a Clock component to your viewer. It should appear under "Non-Visible Componenets". Go to Properties and set the TimeInterval property to 500 milliseconds. This makes the mole move every half second.

7. Add to MoveMole

Click Blocks.

Click on Procedures. Drag the procedure then do block to your Viewer. Rename it to "MoveMole".

8. Add set ImageSprite1.X to

Click on ImageSprite1 and click and drag the set ImageSprite1.X to to your to MoveMole block.

8. Add set ImageSprite1.Y to

Click on ImageSprite1 and click and drag the set ImageSprite1.Y to to your to MoveMole block.

10. Add two __ x __ blocks

10. Add two random fraction blocks

Click on Math and add one random fraction block to the first socket of your first __ x __ block. Click Math again and add a second random fraction block to the first socket of your second __ x __ block.

10. Add two __ - __ blocks

Click on Math and add one __ - __ block to the second socket of your first __ x __ block. Click Math again and add a second __ - __ block to the second socket of your second __ x __ block.

10. Add Canvas1.Width and ImageSprite1.Width

Click on Canvas1 and add Canvas1.Width to the first socket of your first __ - __ block. Click ImageSprite1 and add ImageSprite1.Width to the second socket of your first __ - __ block.

10. Add Canvas1.Height and ImageSprite1.Height

Click on Canvas1 and add Canvas1.Height to the first socket of your second __ - __ block. Click ImageSprite1 and add ImageSprite1.Height to the second socket of your second __ - __ block.

10. Add initialize global score to and 0

Click on Variables and drag initialize global score to to your Viewer. Change "name" to "score". Then, click Math and drag a 0 to your initialize global score to block.
