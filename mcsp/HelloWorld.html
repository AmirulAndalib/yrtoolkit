Unit 1 : Hello World Tutorial

Welcome to App Inventor!

App Inventor is a programming development environment for designing apps for phones and tablets using a visual drag-and-drop online interface.

Note that App Inventor is not supported on Internet Explorer browsers. We suggest using Chrome or Firefox.

Designer Window

What you see when you open an App Inventor template is the Designer window. This is where you will design the UI (User Interface) for your app. Explore the Designer window.

Try changing the background color of the Screen in the properties window!

Note that there is a Toggle Tutorial button in the green bar at the top that you can use to open and close this tutorial pane:

My Projects

Try clicking on My Projects at the top to go to your list of projects and then click on HelloWorldTemplate to get back into this project. You will have create projects in this class.

Connect Mobile Device

Let's connect your mobile device to your App Inventor Project.

Click here if you don't have a device and here if you don't have WiFi available.

1. Download Companion App

Download and install the MIT AI2 Companion App on your mobile device if you have not already done so. On Android tablets and phones, it is available free from the play store, just search for App Inventor and you will see the MIT AI2 Companion. You need to install the MIT AI2 Companion only once, and then leave it on your phone or tablet for whenever you use App Inventor.

If you can't use the Play Store click here.

2. Connect to the Companion

In your App Inventor project, click on the Connect menu at the top and choose AI Companion.

Start up the AI Companion app on your mobile device and click on Scan the QR code (or enter the text code) by pointing your mobile device's camera to the computer screen to read the displayed QR code.

If you have problems, make sure your mobile device and your computer are on the same WiFi or other network. See the Troubleshooting page or try another method of connecting your device such as USB or using the emulator.

Hello World!

The classic first program in any programming language is Hello World! This app will say hello when you click a button.

1. Add a Button

Drag in a button into your UI (User Interface).

2. Design Button

In the button's properties, change its Text to say Click Me. Change its Background and Text Colors.

3. Drag in a Notifier

Drag in and drop a Notifier component from the left UI Palette to the middle area. This is an non-visible component that will go to the bottom of the screen. The Notifier will be used for output.

4. Switch to the Blocks Editor

Click on the Blocks button to switch to the Blocks editor.

The Blocks editor is where you add the code to make your button and other UI components work! Tou can go back and forth between Design and Blocks at any time in your project. Notice that every UI component you added such as Button1 and Label1 now have code blocks that you can use. There are also built-in coding blocks that are always available.

5. Add when Button.Clicked

Click Button1 and click and drag the when Button1.Clicked block out to the middle.

6. Add Notifier Alert

Click Notifier1 on the left and click and drag the Notifier1.alert block out and put it inside the when Button1.Clicked block that it already in the middle. Then, click on the Built-in Text drawer and drag in the first block, an empty Text block, at the top, and type in "Hello World!" inside it.

7. Try your App

Your app should already be connected to your mobile device through AI Companion, so you can try clicking the button on your mobile device to see the Hello World! message pop up on the screen. If it is not connected, click on Connect/AI Companion. If it is frozen, try Connect/Reset Connection and then try Connect/AI Companion again. You can also stop the running app on your device by clicking the Overview button (next to home and back) on your device and select stop app if needed.
